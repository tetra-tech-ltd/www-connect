The problem

Your data doesn't agree with itself.

The same person shows up a dozen different ways across your systems — a misspelt surname here, a nickname there, a transposed date of birth, an address from three years ago. Exact-match lookups miss them, and every miss is a duplicate record, an unseen risk, or a citizen asked to repeat themselves.

Misspellings & typos

Smith, Smyth, Smithe — one keystroke apart, invisible to exact match.

Aliases & nicknames

William, Bill, Billy, Will — the same person, four different records.

Transposed dates

04-12 or 12-04? A flipped date of birth breaks a lookup entirely.

Matching

One query. Every variation, ranked.

Tetra Connect blends several matching strategies into a single search, then ranks every candidate by a calculated match score — so the right person surfaces at the top, even from imperfect data.

Fuzzy & typo-tolerant

Finds records despite misspellings, extra characters and transposed letters — Jhon still finds John.

Phonetic matching

Catches names that sound alike but are spelled differently — Smith and Smyth, Catherine and Kathryn.

Nicknames & synonyms

Knows William is also Bill, Billy and Will — with a built-in library you can extend with your own.

Date-of-birth tolerance

Matches close and partial dates within a range you control, scored by how near they fall.

Real-time results

Sub-second responses whether you're searching thousands of records or tens of millions.

Ranked, filtered output

Every candidate scored and ordered, with a minimum threshold that filters out the noise.
